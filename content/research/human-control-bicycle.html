--- title: Human Control of a Bicycle subtitle: what about bears? description: what I studied for my phd degree startdate: !!timestamp '2003-06-01 10:00:00' enddate: !!timestamp '2003-12-31 10:00:00' --- {% mark image -%} {% filter restructuredtext %} .. image:: {{ media_url('images/zambulance.jpg') }} {% endfilter %} {%- endmark %} {% mark excerpt -%} {% filter restructuredtext %} My graduate work at UC Davis was rounded out with a project that focused on identifying the control actions and model employed by a human during simple manuevers. I constructed a heavily instrumented bicycle and collected a great deal of data during a series of experiments in which the rider attempted to balance a bicycle under lateral perturbations. This data was then used to identify both a plant and control model of the underlying system and validate our hypostheses about the rider's control structure. The work is detailed both in `my dissertation`_ and `my lab's website`_. .. _my dissertation: http://moorepants.github.com/dissertation .. _my lab's website: http://biosport.ucdavis.edu/research-projects/bicycle {% endfilter %} {%- endmark %}
